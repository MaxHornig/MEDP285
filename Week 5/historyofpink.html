    History of the Color Pink

    Origin
    The color pink was recognized as a concept in 800 B.C. in Homerâ€™s Odyssey.
    The term was coined in the 17th century, first appearing in the English dictionaries of the time.
    In the mid-18th century, pink was a preferred color among aristocrats, due to the expense of the dye.
    In the mid-20th century, men started to wear darker colors. Bright and pastel colors like pink were rebranded as feminine.
    This connotation extended to girls in the 1980s, and then shifted back to being somewhat gender neutral in the 2000s and onward.
